Ophthalmology

Ophthalmologists provide comprehensive eye care, including medical, surgical and optical care.

Early Decision (MS1 / MS2)

  • Get involved in ophthalmology activities, e.g., UB Sight Savers, Ophthalmology Interest Group, and Unite for Sight
  • Shadow and ophthalmologist in his or her office and OR, even a few hours can give you an idea of a typical day
  • Attend opthalmology grand rounds at the Ross Institute, Thursdays at 7am (September through May). Location: Second floor conference room
  • Read about ophthalmology: Good starting point is eyewiki.aao.org
  • Basic Research: In MS1 and MS2, you have time to get involved in a long-term basic science project. Get in touch with Dr. Steven Fliesler (fliesler@buffalo.edu), Lisa Prince-Baker (lsprince@buffalo.edu), or Dr. Jack Sullivan (jackmsullivanmdphd@yahoo.com).
  • Clinical Research: Clinical studies are also an option, contact Dr. Sandra Sieminski (smfernando17@gmail.com).
  • Summer research after MS1 is ideal. Apply for research grants through the university or through outside funding source.

Later Decision (MS3 / MS4)

    Ophthalmology rotation at home school:
  • Preferably chose a 4 week elective rotation during medicine or surgery during MS3
  • Review introductory ophthalmology book (Basic Ophthalmology from AAO provided prior to rotation, contact Elaine Taylor (etaylor@buffalo.edu))
  • The rotation can be tailored to observe specific subspecialties. There is opportunity to present at weekly grand rounds during rotation to gain exposure to faculty.
  • Contact ophthalmology clerkship director Dr. Sandra Sieminski (smfernando17@gmail.com) to prepare for the rotation
  • If you have completed a 4 week MS3 ophthalmology rotation, you may still enroll in an an MS4 ophthalmology rotation if it involves a different experience (for example, cornea/glaucoma in MS3 and retina/pediatrics in MS4)
    Research:
  • Basic science projects will be difficult to initiate and complete, therefore you are usually limited to clinical projects
  • Research electives months during 3rd and 4th year can be utilized to complete projects
  • Case reports/series are a rapid route to initiate and complete a paper, if time is limited
  • If you have not published your data at the time of your application, make sure to list your ongoing projects
  • Present poster or paper at a scientific meeting (i.e., AAO or ARVO), if the opportunity arises
    Ophthalmology away rotations:
  • Not required, but usually performed early during MS4
  • Important to have some clinical experience prior to the away rotation, as you will have a short time to make an impression on your preceptors
  • Typically, the institution provides a courtesy interview
    Letters:
  • 3 required, more than 1 from ophthalmologists expected. Other suggested fields include Surgery, medicine, or related subspecialties. Less desirable, but acceptable letters from fields such as Pathology, radiology, or psychiatry
  • Ensure that your letter writers know you well, and give them adequate time to prepare a letter
  • You may obtain a letter from an away rotation if completed early enough
